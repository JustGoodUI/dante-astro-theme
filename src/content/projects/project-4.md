## Project Overview

HERA is an internal web-based platform designed to improve material control, traceability, and operational visibility across production-related workflows. The system was created to manage the lifecycle of controlled materials from order creation to supply, return, adjustment, and completion, while keeping a detailed transaction history for reporting and audit purposes. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

The platform helped standardize material handling processes by ensuring that required quantities were validated, tracked, and reconciled through structured operational flows. It also provided role-based access, reporting capabilities, and integration with external data sources to support accurate execution and better decision-making. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3} :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

## Objectives

1. Centralize material supply and return workflows in a single platform.
2. Improve traceability of controlled materials across operational stages.
3. Reduce manual tracking errors through scanning, validation, and workflow controls.
4. Provide audit-ready reporting for material movements and user actions.
5. Strengthen inventory control by validating quantities, returns, adjustments, and order completion. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

## Key Features

### 1. Order Management
The system allowed users to create, edit, monitor, cancel, adjust, and complete material orders. Orders could be created based on production references, batch/work-order information, and required quantities, giving operations teams better visibility into open and pending requests. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

### 2. Material Supply and Return Control
Users could load materials, supply them to the operation, receive returns, and manage adjustments when returned quantities did not match expected values. This helped ensure better reconciliation between requested, supplied, returned, and adjusted quantities. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

### 3. Workflow Monitoring
The platform included monitoring screens for order status, material movement, and pending actions. Orders could be tracked through multiple states, allowing teams to identify bottlenecks, aging orders, and incomplete returns. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

### 4. Drawer and Controlled Storage Monitoring
The application included modules to manage material entry, removal, and balance tracking in controlled storage locations. These screens supported visibility into available quantities, pending inputs, and output transactions. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

### 5. Rework and Categorization Flow
A dedicated process allowed users to register serial-level categorization and track rework-related material conditions. The platform supported controlled registration, search, approval flow for special cases, and final process closure. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

### 6. Reporting and Audit Trail
The system provided multiple operational reports, including transaction logs, order logs, storage movement history, and serial-level reports. This gave teams strong visibility into material history, user actions, and traceability events. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

### 7. Catalog and Configuration Management
The application also included catalogs and maintenance options for project lines, material classifications, and other operational configurations required for day-to-day execution. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
